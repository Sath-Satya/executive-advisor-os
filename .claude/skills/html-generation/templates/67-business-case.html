01

Executive Summary

Summary

Meridian Health processes 4.2 million medical claims annually. Approximately 38% require manual review due to legacy adjudication system limitations, costing $8.6 million per year in labor and generating a 14-day average resolution cycle. This business case recommends investing $4.2 million in a cloud-native AI claims automation platform (ClaimAI Pro) to automate 85% of routine adjudications.

The investment returns a 5-year NPV of $3.66 million at a 9% discount rate, an IRR of 38.4%, and a simple payback period of 2.3 years. Annual run-rate benefits of $2.65 million begin from Year 2.

Recommendation: Approve the $4.2M capital authorization and proceed to vendor contracting and implementation planning in Q2 2026.

02

Problem Statement

Current State

Meridian’s claims adjudication process relies on a 12-year-old on-premises system purchased in 2014. The platform lacks machine learning capabilities and requires claims examiners to manually review all complex, duplicate, and out-of-network claims.

  • 38% of 4.2M annual claims (1.59M claims) require manual review
  • Average examiner cost per manual claim: $5.40
  • Total annual manual adjudication cost: $8.59M
  • Average resolution cycle: 14 calendar days (CMS target: 30 days; industry best: 3–5 days)
  • Denial-and-appeal rate: 12.4% (industry benchmark: 7.1%)
  • Estimated overpayment and fraud exposure: $2.1M annually

Business Impact

The slow resolution cycle directly affects member satisfaction (−18 NPS vs. regional peers) and creates regulatory risk. CMS flagged Meridian in Q3 2025 for excessive denial appeal rates, raising Star Rating risk. Continued status quo is estimated to cost $7.0M over 5 years in compounding infrastructure and staffing expense.

Implementation Plan

    Procurement & Contracting
