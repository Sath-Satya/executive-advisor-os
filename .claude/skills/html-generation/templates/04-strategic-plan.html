01 — Vision

Where We Are Going

"By 2029, Meridian Health will be the first choice for 500,000 patients across our four-state region — not because we are the largest network, but because we are the one they trust most to know them, reach them, and care for them."

This vision is grounded in three realities: patients are demanding more from their health providers, technology has finally made personalised care at scale possible, and Meridian has the clinical foundation and community relationships to win if we act with speed and discipline.

02 — Strategic Pillars

Three Pillars of Growth

Pillar 01

Digital Patient Experience

Build a seamless digital touchpoint for every stage of the patient journey — from first search to post-discharge follow-up.

Pillar 02

Operational Excellence

Reduce administrative burden by 40% through intelligent automation, freeing clinical staff for higher-value patient care.

Pillar 03

Network Expansion

Enter two new markets by end of 2028 via targeted acquisitions or de novo clinic openings in high-growth suburban corridors.

04 — Milestones

Three-Year Roadmap

  • Q4 2026 — Foundation

    Platform Architecture Approved & FHIR Compliance Delivered

    Board approves $8.4M platform investment. FHIR R4 adapter deployed. Engineering team upskilled.

  • H1 2027 — Build

    Patient Portal v2.0 Launch & AI Scheduling Pilot

    New patient portal live in all primary markets. AI scheduling assistant pilot with 3,000 patients. NPS baseline established.

  • H2 2027 — Scale

    Full AI Rollout & Revenue Cycle Phase 1

    AI scheduling at 100% network coverage. Revenue cycle automation Phase 1 delivers 18% reduction in claim denials.

  • 2028 — Expand

    Phoenix Market Entry & 400K Patient Milestone

    First Phoenix clinic operational by June 2028. Active patient count exceeds 400,000. Preparing for 2029 second market entry.

  • 2029 — Vision Realised

    500K Patients, Second Market, Clinical Decision Support Live

    Vision target achieved. Meridian is the highest-NPS health network in four-state region.
