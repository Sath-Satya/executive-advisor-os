Highlights & Lowlights

Lowlights

  • Claim denial rate at 12.4% vs 9.0% target ICD-11 migration issues identified. Revenue cycle automation vendor delivered 6 weeks late. Remediation plan in place.
  • Gross margin 80bp below plan Driven by higher-than-planned clinical staffing to cover Phoenix launch preparation. Expected to normalise in Q4.
  • 55+ digital adoption lagging significantly This cohort is 38% of our billable volume. UX accessibility sprint starts 1 August with a 90-day target of 25% digital share.

Context & Commentary

The core investment thesis is tracking ahead of expectations on the metrics that matter most for long-term value — patient acquisition, NPS, and revenue. The digital adoption gap in the 55+ cohort is our most important near-term operational challenge. We have diagnosed the root cause (UX complexity, not awareness or willingness) and have a specific engineering sprint beginning 1 August with clear success criteria.

On the balance sheet: our 14-month runway is adequate, but we are beginning Series C relationship-building now rather than waiting until Q4. We expect to enter formal raise mode in Q4 2027, targeting $22-28M at a post-money valuation reflecting our revenue growth trajectory and the digital platform's strategic option value. We will share a detailed Series C narrative at the September board meeting.
