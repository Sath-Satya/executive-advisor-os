Context

Meridian Health's current patient-facing stack runs on a 2019-era monolith with a 94-day mean time to feature delivery. Three competitor platforms operating on modern cloud-native architectures are shipping weekly. Technical leadership must evaluate whether to modernise in-place, migrate to a new platform, or acquire a solution — and present a recommendation to the Board by 30 August.

Technical Findings

  • F-01
    Monolith has a 14-module blast radius A single deployment touches all 14 patient-facing modules simultaneously. Six production incidents in 2025 stemmed from unrelated changes to the billing module triggering cascading failures in appointment scheduling.
    High
  • F-02
    Salesforce Health Cloud foundation is production-ready Existing SFHC implementation covers 80% of required patient engagement capabilities. A targeted integration layer can bridge the remaining gap within the estimated 14-week window at lower risk than a full rebuild.
    Opportunity
  • F-03
    API surface is not FHIR R4 compliant Current HL7 v2.x integrations will require a mandatory migration layer before the CMS January 2027 deadline. Building this as part of the platform modernisation is 40% cheaper than a standalone retrofit project.
    Medium
  • F-04
    Team capability gap is addressable in 8 weeks 12 of 23 engineers have cloud certifications. A structured upskilling sprint on Azure Container Apps + API Management closes the remaining gap without external hires, preserving institutional knowledge.
    Manageable

Recommendation

Technical Leadership Recommendation

Adopt a strangler-fig migration pattern over 18 months. Extract the three highest-traffic modules (scheduling, patient messaging, care plan) as independently deployable services in the first 6 months. Retain the monolith for non-critical modules until services are proven stable. Do not pursue a full rewrite — the risk profile does not justify the timeline.

Estimated outcome: mean feature lead time falls from 94 days to under 12 days by month 12; uptime target 99.95% by month 18.
