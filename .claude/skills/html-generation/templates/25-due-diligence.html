Section 01 • Grade: B+

Financial Due Diligence

Financial review was conducted over a four-week period based on three years of audited financial statements (FY21–FY23), FY24 management accounts through Q3, and a revenue bridge prepared by management. Northwind's CFO advisory team performed independent revenue quality analysis on a sample of 47 customer contracts representing 68% of ARR.

Revenue Quality

Management reports $18.4M ARR as of September 2024. Northwind's independent review confirms approximately $16.8M as qualifying ARR under a conservative recognition policy (excluding pilot contracts under $50K annual contract value and contracts with unusual termination clauses). The $1.6M gap is not a red flag in isolation but warrants disclosure in the Series B investor documents.

Medium Risk — Revenue Quality

Management's ARR calculation includes $1.6M in pilot and non-standard contracts that do not meet Northwind's ARR quality criteria. Recommend adjusting the deal valuation model to use $16.8M qualifying ARR. No evidence of intentional misrepresentation — this appears to be a definitional difference rather than a misstatement.

Unit Economics

Gross margin on software revenue is 74%, which is appropriate for the sector and consistent with the company's infrastructure spend profile. Customer acquisition cost (CAC) payback period is 19 months, slightly above the 14-month median for comparable SaaS companies at this stage. The extended CAC payback is primarily attributable to a higher-touch enterprise sales motion that management is actively optimizing.

Section 02 • Grade: B

Legal Due Diligence

Legal review was conducted by Northwind's outside counsel in partnership with the company's general counsel. Review covered corporate structure, material contracts, intellectual property, employment agreements, outstanding litigation, and regulatory compliance status.

High Risk — Outstanding Litigation

The company has an unresolved accounts payable dispute with former technology vendor CloudBuild Inc. totaling $1.4M. CloudBuild filed a demand letter in July 2024; no court action has been initiated. Management believes the dispute is defensible based on service level failures documented in the SOW. However, the contingent liability is not reflected in the FY24 balance sheet, which may require an adjustment to the closing balance sheet. This condition must be resolved or adequately indemnified prior to close.

Intellectual Property

IP ownership review confirmed that all material technology is owned by the company. Three of eleven founders and key employees executed IP assignment agreements at incorporation; the remaining eight have agreements dated within 18 months of the first commercial product release. Northwind's counsel assessed the IP assignment chain as clean with no material gaps.

Customer Contracts

A sample of 12 enterprise customer contracts (representing 81% of ARR) was reviewed. Standard terms are favorable: 12-month minimum commitments, annual price escalators averaging 4%, and no right-to-audit provisions that would create material compliance burden. Two contracts contain most-favored-nation pricing clauses that could create pricing pressure if the company pursues volume discounting with new enterprise customers.

Section 03 • Grade: A-

Technology Due Diligence

Technology review included a two-day on-site architecture review with the engineering team, code review of the core AI inference pipeline, infrastructure audit, security assessment, and scalability modeling. Review was conducted by a Northwind technical advisor with 12 years of AI infrastructure experience.

The technical foundation is strong. The core product is built on a well-architected multi-tenant SaaS platform with appropriate separation between tenant data contexts. The AI inference layer uses a model-agnostic abstraction that allows model substitution without application-layer changes — a meaningful architectural advantage that reduces vendor lock-in risk.

Infrastructure is primarily Azure-based with appropriate autoscaling configurations. The engineering team demonstrated competent use of infrastructure-as-code (Terraform) and a mature CI/CD pipeline. Test coverage at 78% is above the industry median for the product's stage.

Medium Risk — Scalability

The database schema has not been tested at greater than 50,000 concurrent active users. Management projects reaching this threshold within 18 months of funding. Northwind's technical advisor recommends a pre-Series B load test at 2x projected peak load before the company's next major enterprise contract execution.

Section 04 • Grade: B

Team Assessment

The founding team presents strong domain expertise combined with relevant prior operating experience. The CEO has one prior successful exit (acquired by a publicly-traded healthcare IT company in 2019). The Chief Medical Officer brings 18 years of clinical informatics experience at two major health systems, which is a material differentiator for enterprise sales in the regulated healthcare market.

High Risk — Key Person Dependency

The VP of Engineering and two senior backend engineers (who collectively designed the core AI inference architecture) are not currently under retention agreements tied to the fundraising event. Market compensation for engineers of this profile is highly competitive. Northwind strongly recommends executing retention agreements with vesting tied to the Series B close prior to investment. Failure to do so creates meaningful execution risk in the 18-month product roadmap.
