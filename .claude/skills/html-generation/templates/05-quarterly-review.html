Q2 Results — Wins, Misses & Risks

  • Win
    Patient portal v2.0 launched on schedule Delivered to all primary markets by 15 June. 22,000 patients registered in first 30 days — 46% above forecast.
  • Win
    NPS improved 17 points year-over-year Driven by portal launch and AI scheduling pilot in Phoenix Metro. 68% of surveyed patients cited scheduling ease as primary driver.
  • Win
    Revenue exceeded target by $2.2M Outperformance from two new specialist clinics opened in Q1 achieving patient capacity ahead of plan.
  • Miss
    Digital appointment share below target Portal adoption concentrated in age 30-45 segment. 55+ adoption at 8% vs target 22%. Requires UX simplification sprint.
  • Miss
    Claim denial rate worsened ICD-11 coding errors post-migration drove 38% of denials. Revenue cycle automation Phase 1 delayed to Q3 due to vendor capacity.
  • Risk
    Phoenix market entry timeline at risk Site permitting delayed by 8 weeks. Contingency plan: temporary leased space identified. Final decision required by 15 August.

Q3 Outlook

Q3 presents a bifurcated risk profile. The NPS trajectory and revenue momentum are strong and expected to continue. The claim denial and digital adoption gaps require targeted interventions in weeks 1-4 of Q3 — if both are addressed promptly, the full-year plan remains on track. The Phoenix timeline risk is the highest-priority decision for the CEO and Board in August.
