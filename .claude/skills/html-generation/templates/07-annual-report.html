01 — Letter from the CEO

A Year of Decisions That Will Define a Decade

Fiscal year 2027 was not the most comfortable year in Meridian Health's history. We made a $8.4 million bet on our digital future when many of our peers were waiting for certainty. We launched a new patient platform, reorganised our technology team, and navigated an ICD-11 migration that cost us more than we planned. We did all of this while growing revenue 18% year-over-year and achieving the highest patient satisfaction scores in our 22-year history.

I am not reporting a perfect year. The claim denial rate story is one I will address directly with our investors and our team. What I will say is this: we now have the root cause fully understood, the fix is in flight, and the underlying capability we built — a modern, API-first platform — means we will never be in this position again.

The year ahead is one of execution. The platform is built. The teams are aligned. The strategy is approved. 2028 is the year we prove that the investment was worth it.

03 — Digital Transformation

Platform Launch & First-Year Results

The patient portal launched on 1 June 2027 and registered 44,000 users within its first 45 days — 46% ahead of forecast. By year-end, portal monthly active users had reached 68,000, representing 22% of our total active patient base. The platform's AI scheduling assistant reduced average appointment wait time by 22%, which was the primary driver of the 11-point year-over-year NPS improvement.

The 55+ adoption challenge — digital appointment share at 8% for this cohort versus our 22% target — was the primary digital underperformance of the year. A targeted UX accessibility programme launched in August 2027 has improved this to 14% by year-end, with a 25% target by end of Q2 2028.

06 — 2028 Outlook

What the Board Has Approved for 2028

The board has approved a 2028 plan built on three commitments: reaching 400,000 active patients through organic growth and the Phoenix market entry, delivering the revenue cycle automation that was deferred from 2027, and completing the FHIR R4 compliance work ahead of the CMS January 2028 deadline.

Revenue guidance for 2028 is $172-180M, reflecting 16-22% growth. The platform investment tailwind is expected to drive operating leverage: gross margin improvement from 34.1% to 37-38% as digital appointment share grows and administrative automation takes effect. Platform-related one-time expenses are substantially behind us.
