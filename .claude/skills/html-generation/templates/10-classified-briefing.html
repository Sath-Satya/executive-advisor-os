SEC. 1

Executive Summary

(S // BOARD)

This briefing assesses the competitive threat landscape facing Meridian Health's digital platform strategy as of Q3 2027. Intelligence gathered from REDACTED sources and open-source analysis indicates that two primary competitors have advanced their platform capabilities beyond previously assessed timelines. The threat to Meridian's patient acquisition and retention in contested markets is assessed as HIGH and time-sensitive.

Meridian's window of asymmetric advantage — the 9-month lead period identified in the August 2026 board briefing — has compressed to an estimated 5-6 months as of the date of this document. Immediate action on digital adoption acceleration and market entry is recommended.

Key Intelligence Facts

  • BlueRidge Health shipped AI scheduling integration in May 2027 — 14 weeks ahead of our estimated timeline.
  • Summit Health has contracted with REDACTED for a 55+ patient UX redesign, suggesting awareness of the same demographic gap we have identified.
  • Regional competitor REDACTED is reported to be in advanced acquisition discussions with REDACTED patient portal platform. If completed, this would give them overnight scale in digital engagement.
  • Meridian's portal NPS advantage (48 vs industry median 31) is assessed as durable for 6-9 months absent further investment.

SEC. 4

Recommended Actions

(S // BOARD)

Based on the threat assessment above, the Strategic Intelligence Group recommends the following immediate actions for board consideration:

1. ACCELERATE — The 55+ accessibility sprint timeline should be compressed from 12 weeks to 8 weeks. If Summit Health goes live in Q4 2027 with a competitive 55+ UX, Meridian's first-mover advantage in this cohort is eliminated. Estimated additional cost to compress timeline: $180K.

2. MONITOR — Establish a formal 30-day monitoring cadence on the REDACTED acquisition. If the transaction closes, a Board-level response briefing should be convened within 72 hours. Pre-brief the Phoenix market entry team on contingency options.

3. MAINTAIN — Current FHIR R4 compliance trajectory is adequate. No acceleration recommended. Competitors falling behind this deadline create a short-term regulatory moat for Meridian in markets where CMS enforcement is active.
