Situation

Meridian Health faces a strategic inflection point. Three competing health networks have launched AI-assisted patient engagement platforms in the past eight months, capturing early adopter share in our primary markets. A decision on platform investment — or deliberate deferral — must be made before the Q3 capital planning cycle closes on 15 August.

Key Findings

  • 01
    Competitor velocity is accelerating BlueRidge and Summit Health each shipped two major platform releases in Q1 2026. Their combined patient portal adoption rate is now 34% versus Meridian's 11%.
  • 02
    Internal capability gap is bridgeable Meridian's existing Salesforce Health Cloud implementation provides a viable foundation. Gap analysis indicates 14 weeks to MVP readiness with current vendor relationships.
  • 03
    Patient demand is documented and growing CAHPS survey data (n = 4,200) shows 68% of patients aged 30–54 would switch providers for a better digital experience. This segment represents 61% of Meridian's billable volume.
  • 04
    Regulatory runway is favorable CMS interoperability rules effective January 2027 mandate FHIR R4 API access. Proactive investment now yields a compliance dividend and avoids penalty exposure estimated at $2.1M annually.

Recommendation

Board Action Required

Authorize Phase 1 platform investment of $3.2M by 1 September 2026 to fund discovery, architecture, and an 8-week MVP build targeting three priority markets. Approve conditional Phase 2 release of $5.2M contingent on MVP net promoter score exceeding 42 and six-month retention above 74%.

Delay beyond Q3 capital freeze forfeits the 9-month lead window and elevates revenue-at-risk from $42M to an estimated $67M by mid-2027.
