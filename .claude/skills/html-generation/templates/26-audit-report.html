Basis for Opinion

Basis for Qualified Opinion

The audit was conducted in accordance with Generally Accepted Auditing Standards (GAAS) and applicable standards issued by the Public Company Accounting Oversight Board (PCAOB) where applicable. We planned and performed the audit to obtain reasonable assurance about whether the financial statements are free from material misstatement, whether due to fraud or error.

Our audit included performing procedures to assess the risks of material misstatement of the financial statements, whether due to fraud or error, and performing procedures that respond to those risks. Such procedures included examining, on a test basis, evidence supporting the amounts and disclosures in the financial statements. Our audit also included evaluating the accounting principles used and significant estimates made by management, as well as evaluating the overall presentation of the financial statements.

Material Weakness — Logical Access Controls

During the audit period, we identified a material weakness in the design and operating effectiveness of the entity's logical access controls over privileged user accounts in the production database environment. Specifically, we found that fifteen terminated employee accounts retained active database access for periods ranging from 14 to 87 days post-termination. No evidence of unauthorized data access was found; however, the control failure creates a material risk of unauthorized access that is not commensurate with the entity's risk tolerance or applicable regulatory requirements. This finding is detailed in AR-2024-003 below.

Audit Findings

Findings and Recommendations

The following findings were identified during the audit. Each finding is classified by severity and includes a recommendation and management's response deadline. Findings are numbered sequentially for tracking purposes.

  • Critical AR-2024-001 Segregation of Duties Failure in Accounts Payable Processing Open

    Condition: The accounts payable manager has the ability to both create vendor records and approve payments to those vendors without a compensating secondary approval requirement. During testing, we identified 14 transactions totaling $218,400 where the same individual created the vendor record and approved the subsequent payment within the audit period.

    Risk

    This control deficiency creates a material risk of unauthorized payments to fictitious or unauthorized vendors. The concentration of capabilities in a single individual constitutes a segregation of duties failure under COSO internal control standards.

    Recommendation

    Implement a two-person approval requirement for all new vendor creation and for all payments to vendors created within the preceding 90 days. Configure the payment management system to enforce this workflow constraint. Complete by {{REMEDIATION_DATE_1}}.

  • Critical AR-2024-002 Missing Encryption at Rest for PII in Secondary Data Warehouse In Progress

    Condition: The secondary analytics data warehouse (Snowflake instance: {{INSTANCE_ID}}) does not have encryption at rest enabled. This instance contains member personally identifiable information (PII) including names, dates of birth, and member identification numbers for approximately 340,000 individuals. Management was unaware that the instance had been provisioned without the encryption flag set during a 2022 infrastructure migration.

    Risk

    Unencrypted PII storage creates material exposure under HIPAA, state privacy laws (including CCPA), and the entity's own data security policy. Discovery by regulators could result in civil penalties.

    Recommendation

    Enable Snowflake Tri-Secret Secure encryption on the secondary warehouse immediately. Conduct a data lineage review to confirm no other unencrypted instances contain PII. Complete immediate remediation within 15 business days.

  • High AR-2024-003 Terminated Employee Accounts Retained Active Database Access In Progress

    Condition: Access control review identified 15 database user accounts belonging to employees who had been terminated during the audit period. Account deactivation was delayed by 14 to 87 days post-termination due to a gap in the offboarding workflow between HR and IT operations. This finding constitutes the material weakness described in the Basis for Opinion section.

    Recommendation

    Implement automated account deactivation triggered by HR system termination event, with a maximum 24-hour delay. Conduct quarterly access recertification reviews. Implement real-time alerting for any access by accounts in terminated status.

  • Medium AR-2024-004 Incomplete Vendor Risk Assessments for Three Critical Vendors Open

    Condition: Annual vendor risk assessments are required by policy for all vendors classified as critical or high-risk. Of 18 vendors in these classifications, 3 (16.7%) had not received required annual risk assessments as of the audit date. The assessments were 90, 142, and 203 days overdue respectively.

    Recommendation

    Complete overdue vendor risk assessments within 30 days. Implement automated tracking in the vendor management system with escalation alerts at 30, 60, and 90 days past due date.
