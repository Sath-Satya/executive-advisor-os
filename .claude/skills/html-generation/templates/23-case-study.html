Client Overview

About Meridian Health Systems

Meridian Health Systems is a regional managed care organization serving 340,000 members across the Pacific Northwest. With a network of 2,200 contracted providers and an annual prior authorization (PA) volume exceeding 180,000 requests, Meridian's utilization management (UM) team is the operational backbone of its medical cost control program.

Like most health plans of its size, Meridian operated a primarily manual PA review process: clinical reviewers accessed multiple disparate systems — claims history, clinical guidelines, physician notes — to gather the evidence needed for each determination. The process was slow, inconsistent, and a growing source of provider and member friction.

The Challenge

A Review Process That Could Not Scale

By the end of FY2023, Meridian's UM team faced a confluence of pressures that made the status quo unsustainable. PA request volume had grown 22% year-over-year, driven by expanded specialty drug coverage and a new behavioral health benefit tier. Average review time had extended to 72 hours, against an internal target of 48 hours and a regulatory ceiling of 72 hours for non-urgent requests.

Root Cause: Clinical reviewers were spending an average of 41 minutes per PA request — not reviewing clinical evidence, but locating and consolidating it from six disconnected source systems. Less than 30% of review time was spent on the actual clinical determination.

Specific Pain Points

Stakeholder interviews and time-motion studies identified three primary failure modes: inconsistent evidence gathering across reviewers, high cognitive load from context-switching between systems, and a growing backlog that required costly overtime and contractor augmentation to manage.

A secondary issue — denial rate variance of up to 18 percentage points across reviewer cohorts for the same procedure codes — indicated that the underlying process lacked sufficient standardization to produce reliable, defensible determinations.

The Solution

AI-Augmented Evidence Surfacing with Clinical Guardrails

The engagement centered on one principle: augment the clinical reviewer, not replace them. The AI system was designed to handle the evidence retrieval and consolidation work — the 41-minute problem — while leaving all clinical judgment and final determinations with the credentialed reviewer.

  • 01
    Unified Evidence Retrieval Layer A structured data API aggregated claims history, clinical notes, lab results, and prior authorization history from six source systems into a single, PA-scoped evidence bundle. Median retrieval time: 47 seconds.
  • 02
    Guideline-Anchored Summarization An AI model parsed the evidence bundle and mapped clinical facts to the relevant InterQual or MCG guideline criteria, producing a structured summary that identified which criteria were met, which were unmet, and which required reviewer assessment.
  • 03
    Confidence-Scored Recommendation The system generated a provisional recommendation (approve/pend/deny) with a calibrated confidence score. Low-confidence cases were automatically flagged for senior reviewer attention rather than being routed to the standard queue.
  • 04
    Full Audit Trail and Override Logging Every AI-generated summary and recommendation included a provenance record — which sources were used, which guideline version was applied, and what the AI confidence score was. All reviewer overrides were logged with reason codes for quality review.

Results

Measurable Outcomes at 6-Month Post-Launch

Results were measured against a pre-launch baseline derived from six months of historical PA data. The comparison was adjusted for seasonal volume variation using a matched-period control from the prior year.

44% Reduction in Denial Rate

From 28.4% to 15.9% denial rate across all procedure categories. Effect strongest in musculoskeletal (52% improvement) and behavioral health (61% improvement).

4 min Evidence Surfacing Time

Reduced from 41 minutes (manual) to 3 min 52 sec (AI-augmented) median per request. 90th percentile: 7 minutes vs. 68 minutes.

$2.1M Annual Cost Avoidance

From reduced overtime, contractor spend, and downstream appeals processing. Excludes medical cost impact of improved determination consistency.

91% Reviewer Satisfaction

Based on post-launch survey of 47 clinical reviewers (n=42 responses, 89% response rate). Pre-launch baseline was 41%.
