Investment Thesis

Why We Invest

Meridian Health processes 4.2 million medical claims annually. Manual adjudication on 38% of claims consumes $8.6M per year in examiner labor, generates a 14-day resolution cycle (2.8× industry best practice), and exposes the organization to $2.1M in annual fraud and overpayment risk. This investment thesis rests on three pillars:

1
Structural cost reduction, not headcount reduction. AI-assisted adjudication reduces the per-claim cost from $5.40 to $0.62 on the 85% auto-adjudicated population. The 18 redeployed FTEs shift to complex case review and provider relations — higher-value activities with direct member satisfaction impact.
2
Regulatory risk mitigation. CMS flagged Meridian in Q3 2025 for a 12.4% denial appeal rate (industry benchmark: 7.1%). Automated adjudication with built-in reason-code capture reduces appeals at source. Estimated Star Rating benefit: +0.5 points, worth $3–5M in quality bonus payments annually.
3
Platform foundation for Phase 2 predictive analytics. The SaaS platform selected (ClaimAI Pro) includes an ML pipeline extensible to predictive denial prevention and utilization management. Phase 2 feasibility study authorized for Q3 2026; preliminary estimate: $800K–$1.2M additional annual benefit.

IC Recommendation
Approve: $4,200,000 Capital Authorization

The Investment Committee finds that the Claims Automation Initiative presents a compelling financial and strategic case. With an IRR of 38.4% substantially exceeding the 9% WACC, a 5-year NPV of $3.66M, a 2.3-year payback, and a minimum-viable realization threshold of 63% (well below base case), the investment exhibits strong downside protection alongside meaningful upside.

The primary risks (HIPAA exposure, change management) are manageable and standard for enterprise SaaS deployments at this scale. Mitigations are costed and included in the budget.

The committee recommends approval subject to: (1) executed vendor contract with BAA and SOC 2 certification prior to data transfer, and (2) monthly steering committee reporting for the first 12 months post go-live.
