# The Decade We Stopped Predicting and Started Building

**Meridian Health All-Hands &nbsp;|&nbsp; January 2026**

*CEO Annual Address — Sasha Reyes*

<!--
SPEAKER NOTES — SLIDE 1 (Title)
Walk on stage without preamble. The title is your opening line — read it, pause, let it land. Do not start with "Good morning, everyone, thanks for being here." That is how every other keynote starts. You are not every other keynote.

Set the tension immediately: we have spent years predicting where healthcare AI was going. In 2026, we are no longer predicting — we are shipping. This year is the year we close the gap between vision and execution.

Tone: confident, warm, direct. This is a room full of people who built Meridian. Honor that before you challenge them.

If there is a live audience, pause to let the energy settle before advancing. If this is recorded, a brief camera cut to the audience before returning to slides works well here.
-->

## The Number That Changed Everything

# 51%

The reduction in manual claims review time delivered by our AI platform — measured across 47 health plans, in production, this year.

<!--
SPEAKER NOTES — SLIDE 2 (Stat)
Single number. One message. Do not add context before the number lands. Say "51 percent." Pause three seconds. Then say: "Not in a lab. Not in a whitepaper. In production. At 47 health plans. This year."

This slide resets the audience's understanding of where we are. Most employees know Claims AI exists; fewer realize the scale of impact it is already having. Make the abstraction concrete: 51% reduction means a claims reviewer who used to process 40 cases a day now processes 81. That is a human being getting back half their day.

The business implication: this metric is the reason our Net Revenue Retention is 118%. Customers do not leave products that give them back half their team's time. This is our moat.
-->

## We Made Four Bets — Here Is the Score

**Bet 1: AI-first beats AI-augmented** &nbsp; Result: ▲ Won. Enterprise customers are selecting us specifically because we are not bolting AI onto a legacy product.

**Bet 2: Remote-first enables best talent** &nbsp; Result: ▲ Won. Engineering fill rate 94%. Voluntary attrition 8.4% vs. 14% industry.

**Bet 3: Value-based care would accelerate** &nbsp; Result: ▲ Won. 14 new state mandates in 2025. Our pipeline grew $42M in 12 months.

**Bet 4: Vertical focus beats horizontal platform** &nbsp; Result: ▲ Won. Three well-funded horizontal competitors pivoted away from health plans in Q3.

<!--
SPEAKER NOTES — SLIDE 3 (Four Bets Scorecard)
This slide is a report card — and it is a good one. Deliver it with confidence but not arrogance. The message is not "we were right and everyone else was wrong." The message is: "We made explicit bets, we tracked them, and the evidence is in. Now we get to make the next set of bets with a higher starting baseline."

The four bets frame is important because it teaches the organization how to think about strategy. We do not make safe predictions. We make explicit, time-bound bets with measurable outcomes. When we win, we know why. When we lose, we learn faster.

After the slide, pause and say: "Four for four. Not bad for a year that started with a global health system under strain and two well-funded competitors circling." Then advance immediately — do not let the room settle into self-congratulation.
-->

## The Culture We Need to Scale

**What got us here:**
Individual brilliance. High tolerance for ambiguity. Move fast and fix it later.

**What gets us to $240M:**
System thinking. Clear ownership. Move fast *and* build it right the first time.

This is not a criticism of what we built. It is an acknowledgment that the next phase of Meridian is bigger than any one person — including me.

<!--
SPEAKER NOTES — SLIDE 8 (Culture Shift)
This is the most important slide in the deck for internal cultural alignment. The first phase of a company is won by individual heroics. The second phase is won by repeatable systems. This is a hard truth that many founders and early employees resist.

Deliver the "what got us here" section with genuine appreciation — look back at specific moments if you can name them. Then make the pivot clean and explicit: "That version of Meridian got us to $183M. The next version has to be different."

The line "bigger than any one person — including me" is important. It demonstrates personal accountability and signals that you are not asking the team to change while exempting yourself. This builds trust and reduces the defensiveness that culture change announcements often trigger.

Allow a moment of silence after this slide. It deserves to land.
-->

## One Commitment — From Me

# Every team that ships on time in Q1 gets a company-wide celebration call — led by me, no exceptions.

<!--
SPEAKER NOTES — SLIDE 9 (CEO Personal Commitment)
This slide does something that most CEO keynotes avoid: it makes a personal, visible, accountable commitment. Not a platitude. Not a value statement. An actual commitment with a trigger condition.

Deliver this with a slight smile but complete seriousness. "I am not joking. If your team ships on time in Q1 — I will be on that call. No conflicts. No reschedules. You earned it."

The mechanism matters: you are creating a visible accountability anchor that the entire company will remember. If Q1 slips, this promise creates a useful tension. If Q1 ships on time, you have a natural moment to celebrate and reinforce the culture you described in slide 8.

This is a technique borrowed from Bezos and Nadella: make the CEO personally visible in delivery milestones, not just strategy announcements.
-->
