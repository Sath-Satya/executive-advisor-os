Commands

Four commands.
That's all you need.

Works the same in Claude Code, Claude.ai, and VS Code. Type the command, the assistant handles the rest.

/new-research
Start a new research topic. The assistant names the folder, asks focused questions, reads your documents, and produces your reports.
→ "CMS 2026 prior auth rule changes"
/continue [name]
Resume exactly where you left off. Full context loaded from memory. Asks what's changed, then goes deeper or updates outputs.
→ /continue cms-2026-prior-auth
/recalibrate [file]
Forgot to include a document? Drop it in the folder, run this command. No questions. Everything regenerates with the new file merged in.
→ /recalibrate final-rule-2026.pdf
/status
See all your research topics, current versions, and what files have been produced. Opens the visual dashboard in your browser.
→ /status

How the questions work

Depth calibrated
to the stakes.

The assistant asks exactly as many questions as the decision warrants — no more, no less. If you drop documents in, it reads them first, then asks only what the documents didn't already answer. Structured multiple-choice questions, not free-form prompts. Your time is worth more picking from concrete options than composing prose.

Always asked — the three core questions
Topic. Goal. Documents.
These three are always asked in free-form, in this order. Everything downstream builds on them.
  • What is the end goal? What decision or action will this research serve?
  • Who is the primary audience — board, specific executive, investors, external partner?
  • Do you have documents to review? (If yes, drop them in and the assistant reads them before asking anything else.)
Complexity classification
One of four tiers — picked automatically
After the three core answers, the assistant classifies your topic into one of four complexity tiers. Each tier runs a different number of structured clarification rounds.
  • Simple — single topic, internal audience, short memo → 1 round of clarification (~2–4 questions)
  • Standard — multi-angle, one audience, one deadline → 2 rounds (~5–8 total)
  • Complex — board-level, multi-stakeholder, regulatory or financial exposure → 3–5 rounds (~9–16 total)
  • Strategic-deep — M&A, market entry, major policy shifts, multi-year bets → 5+ rounds, no ceiling until the picture is genuinely clear
Structured rounds
Multiple-choice, four per round
Each clarification round is a structured multiple-choice interview — up to four questions per round, each with three to five concrete options plus "Other — I'll type it." No open-ended prompts. The assistant asks permission once before the first round, then keeps going as the tier warrants.
No hard cap
You decide when to stop.
The old 8-question maximum is retired. Strategic-deep topics have no ceiling on clarification depth — the assistant keeps asking until the research picture is genuinely clear. But you can say "that's enough" at any point and it stops immediately, writing what it has so far. For Strategic-deep topics, the assistant also auto-invokes the 4-phase first-principles pipeline before producing outputs.

How it's built

Rules the assistant
never breaks

RULE 01
Memory always saved
After every session, memory files are updated. You can always /continue from exactly where you left — even months later.
RULE 02
Versions never overwrite
Every run creates v2, v3, and so on. The v1 from three weeks ago is always there. Nothing is lost.
RULE 03
Dashboard always current
dashboard.html is regenerated after every completed session. One file, always up to date, shows everything.
RULE 04
Deck sizes enforced
Summary deck is maximum 3 slides. Detailed deck is 10–12 slides. These bounds are never violated regardless of content volume.
RULE 05
Questions stay in scope
Every question is about your research. No suggestions to build apps, redesign systems, or do anything outside the research goal.
RULE 06
End goal always clarified
Before any analysis begins, the assistant knows what decision this research serves. Reports without a clear goal are never produced.
